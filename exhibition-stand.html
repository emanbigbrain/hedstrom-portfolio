THE TABLE

In the course interior architecture with visulasation during my studies at Jönköping University we got an assignement to design a exibitionstand. The task was to create an exhibit related to the stick back chair with in the space of 9x4 square meters.

The outcome of my design was a sleek and minimalist exhibit defined by its partition walls in plexiglas that allow light to permeate through. These partitions, strategically placed, provide tantalizing glimpses of what lies beyond, instigating curiosity in the observer.

Within the exhibit, the deliberate and prominent use of the purple color, synonymous with Jönköping University, establishes a direct and recognizable connection to the institution and its program. A playful purple thread weaves through the exhibit, enhancing the flow within the display. This whimsical element follows paths in and out, illustrating the navigation within the space. The incorporation of purple plexiglass not only produces captivating light effects but also amplifies transparency and the infusion of natural light. This design choice contributes to a sense of spaciousness while still maintaining effective separation and contributing to the overall spatial arrangement.

The entire project was crafted in 3D Studio Max with Visualization, ensuring a comprehensive and visually striking representation.
